Straw Hat Crew

The crew currently consists of ten members, being smaller than a typical pirate crew; when he first set sail, Luffy set a goal of gaining ten crewmates. They have one former member, Nefertari Vivi, who sailed with the crew from Reverse Mountain to the Arabasta Kingdom. Despite their small size, the Straw Hats are an extremely diverse group possessing a large variety of backgrounds. Their members' origins range from ordinary islanders and workers to bounty hunters, royalty, criminal gang members, and veteran pirates. Jinbe was once part of the renowned Seven Warlords of the Sea while Nami, Robin, and Franky were active enemies of the crew at certain points with the Arlong Pirates, Baroque Works and the Franky Family respectively. The crew consists of eight humans, one fish-man, and one animal with Devil Fruit-powered human intellect; of the humans, Sanji possesses superhuman modifications given to him at birth, Franky is a cyborg, and Brook possesses a skeleton body after being resurrected by a Devil Fruit power. They range in age from 17 (Chopper) to 90 (Brook); the median age of the crew is 21.

Media

One Piece Manga

  • Created by Eiichiro Oda, first published in 1997 in Weekly Shonen Jump
  • Follows Monkey D. Luffy and his crew, the Straw Hat Pirates, on their journey to find the legendary treasure “One Piece” and become Pirate King.
  • Known for its deep world-building, long-running story arcs, and emotional themes.
  • Still ongoing, with over 1000 chapters and collected into volumes.

One Piece Anime

  • Produced by Toei Animation, began airing in 1999.
  • Closely follows the manga’s story, though often includes filler episodes and extended pacing.
  • Has over 1000 episodes, making it one of the longest-running anime.
  • Adapted into multiple versions, including the original series, remastered editions, and special episodes.

One Piece Movies

  • A series of animated films produced by Toei Animation, separate from the main anime.
  • Usually feature original stories or alternate takes, sometimes supervised by Eiichiro Oda.
  • Famous entries include One Piece: Strong World, Film Z, Film Gold, Stampede, and Film Red.
  • Movies generally focus on big battles, new characters, and high-quality animation, appealing to both fans and newcomers.

Author

Eiichiro Oda is a renowned Japanese manga artist best known as the creator of One Piece, the best-selling manga series in history. Born on January 1, 1975, in Kumamoto, Japan, Oda harbored a passion for manga from a very young age, inspired notably by Akira Toriyama’s Dragon Ball and his fascination with pirate adventures sparked by the animated series Vicky the Viking. By the age of four, Oda had already decided to pursue a career as a manga artist, aiming to create captivating stories for readers around the world. Oda’s professional journey began in his late teens, when he won the prestigious Tezuka Award for his one-shot manga Wanted!, which helped him secure a position as an assistant to prominent manga artists like Nobuhiro Watsuki on Rurouni Kenshin. During this period, Oda developed two pirate-themed one-shots called Romance Dawn, which eventually evolved into the story of One Piece. The series officially began serialization in Weekly Shōnen Jump in 1997 and quickly gained immense popularity. Over the years, One Piece has sold over 520 million copies worldwide, making it a cultural and commercial phenomenon.
